# Tools & Stack

## Positioning

I use these tools to solve **practical business problems**, not as isolated technical experiments. My focus is on building workflows that automate real processes, structuring projects so they are clear and maintainable, and supporting business implementation end to end.

I am a hybrid **business / AI automation** profile. I am comfortable working hands-on with these tools, but I do not position myself as a senior software engineer or infrastructure expert. The value I bring is in connecting business needs to working automations — choosing the right tool for the problem and delivering something usable.

## Core Tools I Use Hands-On

- **n8n** — My main workflow orchestration tool. I use it to connect services, automate multi-step processes, apply conditional logic, and route data between systems with a no-code / low-code approach.
- **GitHub** — Versioning, organizing project files, and documenting work. I use it to keep projects structured, track changes, and present work clearly.
- **Cursor** — My primary development environment. I use it to write and edit files, navigate projects, and work with AI assistance while staying in control of the changes.
- **ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini** — LLMs I use for drafting, interpreting unstructured inputs, structuring information, summarizing, and supporting content and logic. I compare them pragmatically depending on the task.
- **APIs and webhooks** — The connective tissue of my automations. I use APIs to integrate services and webhooks to trigger and pass data between steps in real time.

## How I Think About Tools

I do not start from a tool — I start from the **business problem**. Tool choice depends on:

- **The business problem** — what actually needs to be solved, and the simplest way to solve it.
- **User constraints** — especially whether non-technical users need to interact with the system.
- **Confidentiality** — keeping sensitive data, prompts, and assets protected.
- **Maintainability** — choosing solutions that can be understood, updated, and handed over.
- **Speed of implementation** — delivering practical value quickly rather than over-engineering.

The goal is always a workflow that is **useful, reliable, and appropriate to the context** — not the most technically complex solution possible.
